Santa Cruz

Santa Cruz County was one of the original counties

of California, created in 1850 at the time of

statehood. In the original act, the county was given

the name of "Branciforte" after the Spanish pueblo

founded there in 1797; a major watercourse in the

county, Branciforte Creek, bears this name. Less

than two months later on April 5, 1850, the name

was changed to "Santa Cruz" ("Holy Cross").
